Deep Learning Research: Image Classification on CIFAR-10

This research project involved designing, implementing, and benchmarking three deep learning architectures — a Multilayer Perceptron (MLP), a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN), and ResNet-18 — on the CIFAR-10 image classification dataset using PyTorch.

The primary objective was to evaluate each model's classification performance and generalisation ability while also exploring training efficiency improvements. Regularisation strategies including dropout, weight decay, and data augmentation were applied to mitigate overfitting. The CNN with BatchNormalisation achieved the highest test accuracy of approximately 82%, consistently outperforming both the baseline MLP and ResNet-18 in terms of generalisation on unseen data.

A key contribution of the project was the implementation of training acceleration techniques — specifically Alternating Flip and Identity Initialisation — which reduced training time by approximately 53% (from 68 seconds to 32 seconds per epoch) without any degradation in model accuracy. The project culminated in a full experimental report covering data preprocessing, architecture design decisions, hyperparameter tuning, and comparative loss/accuracy visualisations.
